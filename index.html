Before You Go — Book & Verify
  1. Searoad ferry (Day 7). Pre-book the 7am or 8am vehicle sailing online (~A$106, cheaper than the gate; check in 40 min early). → searoad.com.au
  2. Lorne waterfalls (Day 7). Jan 2026 floods closed several tracks (Erskine base, Sheoak). Re-check parks.vic.gov.au; Teddy's & Marriners lookouts are reliable anchors.
  3. Loch Ard Gorge beach steps (Day 9). Due to reopen Oct 2026 — confirm. Gibson Steps beach reopened Feb 2026.
  4. Dining hours. Cellar doors are mostly lunch-only (Thu–Sun); small-town venues take midweek closures. Check the day-of and book splurge tables well ahead.
Field Notes

Practical, before the romance

Budget tiers
$ casual, under ~A$25pp (bakeries, banh mi, fish & chips, pub counter) · $$ mid, ~A$25–60 · $$$ splurge, A$60+ (hatted & cellar-door degustation).
Weather
Mild, changeable spring (18–22°C; cooler and windier on the coast). Pack layers, a rain shell, and closed shoes for muddy rainforest tracks.
Daylight
Long — sunsets ~8:08pm on the Peninsula to ~8:35pm on the Shipwreck Coast. This is what makes the crowd-free sunset strategy work.
Driving
The Great Ocean Road is winding and slower than it looks — budget generously and watch for wildlife at dawn and dusk. Whale season is over by late November.
